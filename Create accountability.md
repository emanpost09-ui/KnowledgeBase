### **Accountability and Ownership Framework**

|Concept|Definition and Key Actions|Impact & Examples|
|:--|:--|:--|
|**Core Definition**|Building a culture where every person **owns their results**, understands their impact on the bigger picture, and takes action without constant supervision.|**Mindset shift:** Accountability is about **ownership**, not blame.|
|**Key Benefits**|Creates consistency across sites/shifts, reduces errors/rework, and builds trust between TLs, QAs, and agents.|Strengthens client confidence by making results **predictable and measurable**.|
|**Pillar 1: Clear Expectations**|Define specific, measurable KPIs (e.g., QA ≥ 90%, CSAT ≥ 4.5) and use clear SOPs with no "gray areas".|Visibility drives ownership; agents cannot be held accountable for what they don't understand.|
|**Pillar 2: Data Transparency**|Use **dashboards and scorecards** to show performance trends per agent/team and review them in daily stand-ups.|Transparency removes excuses and empowers agents to **self-correct**.|
|**Pillar 3: Consistent Coaching**|Conduct frequent 1:1s focusing on **actionable behaviors** rather than personalities; ensure a two-way dialogue.|Closing the loop by following up on previous coaching converts awareness into action.|
|**Pillar 4: Empowerment & Recognition**|Provide the tools, knowledge, and authority to make decisions; celebrate small wins to link effort to improvement.|**Example:** Empowering agents to resolve billing adjustments up to a certain amount builds confidence.|
|**Pillar 5: Structure & Tracking**|Standardize how performance is recorded using **coaching logs, trackers, and PIP templates**.|Analysts design these simple, data-backed tools to help TLs spot recurring issues early.|
|**Ownership Mindset Culture**|Link metrics to real-world impact (e.g., "1% FCR improvement saves 10 hours of queue time") and recognize proactive reporting.|Encourages people to care about results even when no one is watching.|
|**Team Leader (TL) Role**|TLs create accountability by setting clear goals/consequences and **modeling accountability** by owning their own mistakes.|Consistent follow-through is essential for maintaining the culture.|
|**Business Analyst (BA) Role**|BAs provide factual data that removes guesswork and identify who or what is driving performance variance.|Insights make ownership easier by showing exactly "how to fix it".|
