This quick recall table outlines the **SMART** goal-setting framework, providing definitions, guiding questions, and examples for each component as detailed in the source.

### **SMART Goals Framework**

| Concept        | Definition & Key Characteristics                                                                                | Guiding Questions to Ask                                                                                                                                                                | Example                                                                                                                     |
| :------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Specific**   | Goals should be **clear, specific, and well-defined**, avoiding vague or ambiguous statements.                  | • What exactly do I want to accomplish?<br>• Why is it important?<br>• Who is involved?<br>• What resources or constraints are relevant?                                                | "Increase sales revenue by 10% within the next quarter."                                                                    |
| **Measurable** | Goals must have **measurable criteria** and concrete parameters to track progress and determine success.        | • How will I measure my progress?<br>• What are the quantifiable indicators of success?<br>• How will I know if I have achieved my goal?                                                | "Track monthly sales revenue and compare it against the baseline to measure progress towards the 10% increase target."      |
| **Achievable** | Goals should be **realistic and attainable**, considering available resources, skills, and circumstances.       | • Is the goal within my reach?<br>• Do I have the necessary resources and capabilities to achieve it?<br>• Are there any potential obstacles or limitations?                            | "Based on historical data and market conditions, increasing sales revenue by 10% is attainable within the given timeframe." |
| **Relevant**   | Goals should be **meaningful and aligned** with overall objectives, values, priorities, and larger aspirations. | • Does this goal align with my long-term objectives?<br>• How does achieving this goal contribute to my larger aspirations?<br>• Is this goal relevant to current needs and challenges? | "Increasing sales revenue aligns with the company's growth strategy and supports the overall business objectives."          |
| **Time-bound** | Goals require a **specific timeframe or deadline** to create urgency and accountability.                        | • When do I want to achieve this goal?<br>• Is there a specific deadline or timeframe?<br>• Are there any interim milestones or checkpoints?                                            | "Increase sales revenue by 10% within the next quarter (by the end of September)."                                          |

**Related Roles:** This framework relates specifically to **TL** (Team Lead) and **QA** (Quality Assurance) functions.
