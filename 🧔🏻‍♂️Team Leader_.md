The Team Leader ensures that the frontline team performs effectively and aligns with client expectations and company KPIs. A TL bridges operations, quality, and performance management — translating business goals into day-to-day execution. The TL acts as both a **coach** and a **strategist**.

## Core Responsibilities
- **Coach and Develop team members** Ensuring that each team member:
    - Performs effectively and aligns with client expectations and company KPIs
    - Is equipped with the necessary knowledge and resources to meet and exceed the standards set by the company and the client
- **Manage and drive performance** and hold team members accountable **#accountability**
- **Oversee and guide the activities of the team**
- **Continuously identify opportunities** to improve the customer experience and processes

### Performance Management & Data-Driven Leadership
- **Manage KPI/metrics** Behind every number lies a story. A reason, a behavior, and an opportunity to improve. Metrics only matter when you understand the people and processes behind them. **Use data** not just to measure performance, but to uncover patterns, guide coaching, and drive meaningful results.
- **==Leaders don’t just track KPIs==**; they **analyze patterns, identify root causes, and build connection** (data and behavior) to **drive meaningful change**.
- **[[RCA]] - Identify [[Actionable behaviors]] / Underlying issues**
    - Behaviors that are within our control and can be acted upon
    - **#[[translate data into coaching]]**
    - **#create [[accountability]]**
- **[[Data-driven decision]] making** Use daily/weekly reports to highlight performance gaps and recommend process improvements.
- **Monitor KPIs**: Track team metrics like **AHT** (Average Handle Time), **CSAT** (Customer Satisfaction), **FCR** (First Call Resolution), and adherence.
- **Analyze trends**: Identify performance patterns and underlying causes (e.g., process gaps, knowledge issues, workflow inefficiencies).
- **Drive results**: Implement targeted action plans to improve metrics and meet SLA commitments.

### Coaching and Development
- **[[🦉Me/💀 TL/Coaching]] and [[Performance Management]]**
- Reinforce/Recognize strengths and identify areas of opportunities
- Conduct **one-on-ones**: Review individual agent performance, provide feedback, and set **SMART goals**.
- **Skill enhancement**: Identify training needs through QA reports, error trends, and client escalations, then coordinate with the training team.
- **Motivation & morale**: Use recognition and incentives to boost engagement and reduce attrition.
- Actively engage with team members, providing ongoing feedback and support to help them grow and improve.
- Conduct performance evaluations to assess progress and identify areas for development. Tailor coaching to individual needs.
- **Setting clear expectations and establishing goals**
- Communicate goals and monitor progress regularly.
- Provide guidance and resources to overcome challenges and develop new skills.
- Foster a supportive and collaborative environment where team members learn from each other and share best practices.

**Strengths & Areas of Opportunity Framework** (for feedback/coaching):
- **Strengths**: Start by examining content and identifying strong points. Highlight what is already effective and can be built upon.
- **Areas of Opportunity**: Identify gaps, inconsistencies, unclear explanations, weak arguments, or other aspects that detract from impact. Suggest ways to make content more compelling and valuable.

### Operational Oversight & Activities
- **Call monitoring and evaluation** | **Call calibration** #QA
- **Team huddle and meeting** | **Promoter validation** | **Reporting**
- **DSAT Analysis scrubbing** / **NPS Analysis** / **FCR-Repeat Analysis**
- **Workforce coordination**: Manage shift schedules, break adherence, and attendance to maintain staffing balance.
- **Escalation handling**: Resolve customer issues beyond agent scope while maintaining professionalism and compliance.
- **Process compliance**: Ensure all team members follow SOPs, scripts, and quality guidelines consistently.

### Communication & Reporting
- **Bridge between teams**: Communicate updates from management, clients, and QA to the front line.
- **Client interaction**: Participate in business reviews and share insights on agent performance and operational challenges.
- **Reporting and Documentation**: Generate reports on team performance, project status, etc. Maintain accurate records and provide necessary reports to higher management.

### Continuous Improvement & Collaboration
- **Feedback loop**: Collaborate with QA, Training, and WFM to streamline processes and improve customer experience.
- **Innovation**: Suggest workflow automations, script enhancements, or system optimizations to improve efficiency.
- Encourage a culture of continuous improvement: Identify areas for enhancement, gather feedback, and implement strategies to optimize processes.

### In Summary
The Team Leader is both a **coach** and a **strategist** — ensuring the team delivers consistent performance, adheres to process standards, and supports overall business objectives in the BPO operation.
**Key Mindset**: Connect data with people → Analyze root causes → Translate into actionable coaching → Build accountability → Drive meaningful, sustainable improvement.
