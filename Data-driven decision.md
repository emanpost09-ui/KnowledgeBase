### **Data-Driven Decision Making (DDDM) Overview**

| Concept / Step | Description and Key Actions |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Core Definition** | The practice of using **objective data and analytics**—rather than assumptions or intuition—to make informed operational, performance, and strategic decisions. |
| **The DDDM Formula** | Converting **Metrics** (identifying trends/patterns) into **Insights** (understanding the "why") and then into **Actions** (what to do next). |
| **Step 1: Gather Data** | Collect accurate and relevant data from sources such as QA scores, performance reports (AHT, FCR, adherence), CSAT/NPS feedback, attendance logs, and call analytics. |
| **Step 2: Identify Trends & Patterns** | Analyze data to find repeated errors, outliers (e.g., specific shifts performing differently), correlations (e.g., low empathy affecting CSAT), and trends over time. |
| **Step 3: Analyze & Interpret** | Determine what is and isn't working, what is causing deviations from targets, and where the biggest risks or impacts are located. |
| **Step 4: Formulate Action Plans** | Turn insights into tangible steps like targeted coaching, simplified SOPs, tool improvements, or staffing adjustments based on volume patterns. |
| **Step 5: Evaluate Results** | Validate actions by checking if performance improved (e.g., FCR increase) and identifying if new issues have emerged requiring further control measures. |
| **Benefits of DDDM** | • **Objectivity:** Reduces bias and emotional decision-making.<br>• **Efficiency:** Focuses effort on areas that matter most.<br>• **Accountability:** Allows every decision to be traced back to objective evidence. |
| **Human Element** | True DDDM requires **human judgment with data**. While data reveals *what* is happening, people are needed to understand *why* and *how* to fix it. |

### **Key Data Sources to Monitor**
*   **Quality & KPIs:** QA scores, AHT (Average Handle Time), FCR (First Call Resolution).
*   **Customer Feedback:** CSAT (Customer Satisfaction), NPS (Net Promoter Score), VOC (Voice of Customer).
*   **Operations:** Attendance, schedule adherence, CRM/ticket data.
*   **Advanced Analytics:** Sentiment analysis, silence time, and keyword frequency.
