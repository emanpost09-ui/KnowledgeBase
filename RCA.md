### **Root Cause Analysis (RCA) Framework**

| Concept | Description / Steps / Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Core Definition** | A problem-solving technique used to identify **underlying causes** rather than just addressing symptoms, aiming to prevent recurrence. |
| **1. Define the Problem** | Clearly articulate and define the specific focus for the RCA process. |
| **2. Gather Data** | Collect accurate, reliable, and comprehensive information (facts, figures, reports, customer feedback). |
| **3. Identify Symptoms** | Identify visible indicators that something is wrong to distinguish them from actual root causes. |
| **4. Identify Possible Causes** | Brainstorm a diverse list of potential factors involving individuals with different expertise. |
| **5. Analyze Causes** | Evaluate the likelihood of potential causes; often uses the **5 Whys** technique (asking "Why?" repeatedly). |
| **6. Determine Root Cause(s)** | Narrow the list to the underlying reason(s) that, if addressed, prevent the problem from recurring. |
| **7. Validate Root Cause(s)** | Confirm the accuracy and relationship to the problem through further analysis or expert opinions. |
| **8. Develop Solutions** | Create immediate corrective actions and long-term preventive actions. |
| **9. Implement Solutions** | Put solutions into action with proper planning, coordination, and resource allocation. |
| **10. Monitor & Evaluate** | Continuously assess effectiveness, watch for new issues, and identify areas for further improvement. |

### **Cause-and-Effect Categories (Potential Causes)**
*Used to address the "Effect": Agent errors and performance gaps affecting quality and KPIs.*

| Category                    | Specific Potential Causes                                                                                             |
| :-------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Training & Knowledge**    | Incomplete onboarding, outdated/unclear SOPs, limited knowledge, or poor reinforcement sessions.                      |
| **Communication Skills**    | Weak listening/probing, poor tone or empathy, and language/grammar barriers.                                          |
| **Process & Policy**        | Overfocus on speed, skipped verification steps, failure to follow call flows, or conflicting instructions from QA/TL. |
| **Tools & Technology**      | Multiple disjointed applications, tool downtime/lag, poor UI/UX, or lack of automation.                               |
| **Motivation & Attitude**   | Low engagement/burnout, ignoring feedback, poor attendance, or incentive misalignment (rewarding speed over quality). |
| **Environment & Resources** | High call volume without manpower, unstable WFH setup, shift fatigue, or lack of real-time support from TL/QA.        |

**Related Roles:** RCA is a key activity for **TLs** (Team Leads) and **QA** (Quality Assurance) to drive accountability and effective coaching.
